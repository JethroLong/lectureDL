        • Assessment One

          Posted on: Sunday, 3 September 2017 1:37:27 PM AEST

          What should Assessment One look like?

          It must be fully referenced, with references in the form of footnotes. Written work that is not referenced will not achieve a passing mark. The work submitted must also total 1500 words, plus or minus 10 percent, and be submitted in a single uploaded file.

          We strongly encourage you write in character. We've seen some great speeches already: stirring, emotional, passionately argued from the point of view of the character being played. These will make a great basis for the written assessment. Buff them, add referencing, and make sure you write 1500 words. Remember you can submit two shorter speeches if you prefer, as long as they add up to 1500 words.

          If you strongly prefer to write a regular academic research essay, you may do so. The essay would need to be driven by your character's concerns in the major speech you gave in the game: so a student playing George McGovern would write an essay on why George McGovern opposed the war.

          Ditto for journalists. You may use your material to animate a standard academic research essay. But we urge you to write a newspaper article or television broadcast in character (or two shorter articles/broadcasts).

          Questions? Not sure how to proceed? Please don't hesitate to email me, Liam, or Mia. We want to be as flexible as possible and to accommodate your own preferences, while ensuring rigor and our ability to apply marking standards.

          Consultation hours:

          Ara – Arts West, West Wing 614 – Tuesday after lecture until 4:45

          Liam (all students welcome, not just those in his tutorials) ­– Arts West, West Wing 502 – Wednesday 11-1

        • Academic Integrity

          Posted on: Sunday, 30 July 2017 9:29:45 AM AEST

          The new University-wide module on Academic Integrity is on your LMS home page under the list of classes. I recommend that you read the unit, which is one page and rather clear and straightforward. Take the quiz if you like. But what we will do in this class is spend 5 minutes in tutes doing a quick paper quiz that is rather less intricate than the University-wide one. Basically, then: be sure you are familiar with Academic Integrity policies and responsibilities, and come prepared for a quick quiz in tutes in week 2.
